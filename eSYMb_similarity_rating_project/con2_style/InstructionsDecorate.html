Welcome to this part of the experiment.

In this game, four people will play together split in two groups.

In your group, you will be the DRAWER and another player will be the GUESSER.

In the other group there will also be one DRAWER and one GUESSER.

The overall goal of the game is that the GUESSERS correctly identify the drawings made by the DRAWER from their own group.

There are 8 rounds. In each round, you (the DRAWER) will see 4 patterns (each pattern consists of 6 black lines). One pattern will be pre-selected. You will be asked to draw the selected pattern on the rock surface.
The DRAWER from the other group will also make a drawing, but you will not be able to see it.
Then, the two drawings – one made by you and the other made by the other DRAWER – will be shown to the GUESSERS. Their task will be to guess which drawing was made by the DRAWER from their own group.
At the end of each round, you will learn if the guesses were correct or wrong.

Your objective is to help the GUESSER from your group identify your drawings.

Remember:

  • Your drawing must match the chosen pattern, otherwise it may be rejected, and you will need to redraw it.
  • To draw a line, click and drag your mouse.
  • To move a line, click on its center and drag it to the desired position.
  • To rotate a line, click on one of the ends and drag it to the desired position.
  • To delete a line, double-click on it.
